Pirates of Caribbean Online




If my memory serves, this is the only MMO I have ever played much of, but boy did I play it. This came out shortly after the release of the third Pirates of the Caribbean movie and the conclusion of the original trilogy. I was a young boy fully juiced up on POTC hype and this game was a dream come true. However, do not think this is some crappy movie tie-in game that I just remember fondly because I was a kid. This was one of the most impressive games of my life and the fact that it ran perfectly fine on my 2000s Dell computers still amazes me to this day. I think it was accomplishable because the graphics are not anything special, but still not a detraction. I have no idea how it actually stacks up with other games, but this was probably the biggest-feeling open-world game that I have played and the detail is insane. There are 23 three different islands around the map. A handful are just deserted micro-isles, but they usually have something individual about them. Then the rest are fairly large to massive islands that all have a unique purpose and vibe. Some are controlled by the Davy Jones’ skeleton army, others are controlled by the British or EITC. There are giant towering forts and don’t get me started on the caves. Many islands had insanely large, complex systems of caves filled with enemies and giant magical illusions of Davy Jones commanding his troops. This added a level of depth to each area that I have not seen in many other games. Finally I have to mention that this game also has my favorite in-game events of any that I can remember. I remember standing on the beach with my friends and all the other players in the lobby and watching the clouds turn green and Davy Jones’ ship round the corner, headed for the harbor, ready for invasion. That is one of my favorite gaming memories.
