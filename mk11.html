Mortal Kombat 11




Mortal Kombat 11 delivers what is unequivocally my favorite story among all the titles in the franchise. However, it's not just the narrative that shines; the characters themselves are given a newfound depth, enriching the experience in unprecedented ways. The whole point of the following game was to create new, more fleshed-out versions of the classic characters, but I think this game did it before, perhaps even unintentionally. The standout feature of Mortal Kombat 11 is its ability to breathe life into the characters, it is a delight to witness their growth and evolution as the narrative unfolds. Noob Saibot was sort of the Meta Knight of this game, as in extremely overpowered, but I didn't care. Noob in this is easily my favorite iteration of any character from the franchise. For me personally, his only competition would be Reptile from MK2. Amidst the awe-inspiring hand to hand combat and riveting conflicts, MK11 leaves players pondering how the franchise could possibly escalate from its showdown against the god of time. Yet, it masterfully plants the seeds for the next game, leaving fans eagerly anticipating what's to come. The set up for Mortal Kombat 1 was quite literally, perfect. While the new characters introduced might not match the caliber of some from previous games, they remain enjoyable, well-designed, and carry a unique charm. Mortal Kombat 11 offers a range of gameplay modes, catering to varied preferences. While I didn't delve into every mode extensively, the diverse array of options impressed me, ensuring that players of all tastes could find something engaging. I really liked that they brought back the Krypt gamemode, as it is a testament to the developers' commitment to the franchise's legacy. It's a nod to loyal fans and adds an extra layer of nostalgia and discovery to the game.
