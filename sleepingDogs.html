Sleeping Dogs




This was the last great single-player story game completely marketed on storytelling and fun combat. Newer games always need some sort of special, high tech feature to lean on as a marketing crutch. Whether it is destructible buildings, hyper-realistic player customization, or simply a season pass, games now do not succeed simply as a narrative-driven experience. This game was truly the last of the Mohicans. It is often compared to Yakuza, but to me the biggest difference is it lacks any sort of that Yakuza cartoon goofiness. There are no special powers or over-the-top characters, just one man’s mission. What makes this game one of the best single player narrative-drive experiences is almost all characters and motives being extremely compelling, especially Wei Shen’s. An undercover cop, struggling with doing his job the right way while also being pulled deeper into an underworld that is the key to his personal desires for revenge. If any game was ever set up perfectly for a movie, it would be this one. Every individual side character has such a vibe and personality that they all feel like real factors in this criminal world instead of people just walking on screen and saying a line. You feel that you are slowly being indoctrinated and brought more into the fold with every crime committed or job completed. I love that this game is not afraid to get dark, but never gets overly sad, graphic or gruesome. They tackle some tough topics in the story such as suicide, sexual assault, abuse, torture, etc. When most movies/shows/games bring subjects like this up, it feels like a cheap way to drum up drama. It usually gives the feeling of: did y’all really need to go there? However in this game, they only bring disturbing content up if it actually plays into the story. The last thing I’ll mention may seem a little random and strange, but it is something I do appreciate about this game. I have never been a fan of sex in video games. It has always rubbed me the wrong way and given me the vibes of no-life gamers living vicariously through their game characters. It also often functions in a fashion I find rather disturbing. Take Kingdom Come: Deliverance for example, a game I actually do really like. For some reason, they decided to include sex and relationships in the game and it functions as follows: if every-so-often during your adventures, you come back, visit Theresa, and give her gifts such as rings you’ve taken off people you’ve killed, she’ll have sex with you. I don’t like this reward system, because it teaches that how to have sex with women is just to give them stuff. Theresa’s not a fleshed out character, she simply sits in the same spot every day waiting for you to gift her a ring so it can cut to a graphic digital sex scene. In Sleeping Dogs however, there is no minigame to get women to like you through presents. Wei simply meets a handful of women over the course of the game who take an interest in him and hit on him. The feeling is “ay Wei’s got game,” and not “I got this character to have sex with me,” which to me is just a lot less icky.
