The Sims 3




This is one of the few games I can think of where after a certain point, I myself, came to the realization that I was playing it far too much and it was time to move on. I was obsessed with The Sims 3 from the first time I ever played at my cousin’s house in 2009, to around halfway through high school. It was honestly just too much fun and too addictive. I would spend weeks just sitting in the kitchen of my parents’ house playing this game non-stop. Sometime in high school I made the decision that as much as it pained me, it was time to retire from The Sims 3. Even though I was only around 16, I felt that I was getting to the age where I should probably be working on my own real life, instead of virtual ones. If someone told me my time playing The Sims 3 dwarfs all the other amounts of time I’ve spent on games, I would not be surprised at all. Obviously, you can tell from this website I have a deep love for video games. I have always pushed back against the narratives that video games are a waste of time or not as valuable of an experience as hanging out with friends in real life. However, especially when it comes to a life simulator, you get to a point where it's time to hang up the hat. While this all might sound like a condemnation, I mean it as the highest form of compliment. This game was so fun I had to reevaluate the direction my life was heading, not many games can say that.
