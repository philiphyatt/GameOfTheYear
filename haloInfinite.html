Halo Infinite




Halo Infinite arrived as a breath of fresh air, offering a fantastic reimagining of the beloved franchise's multiplayer. Striking a harmonious balance between classic familiarity and modern updates, the game presented an invigorating new perspective on the Halo universe. The multiplayer mode encapsulated the essence of Halo's roots while seamlessly integrating contemporary elements. It managed to provide the best of both worlds, evoking a sense of nostalgia while embracing innovation. The result was an engaging and immersive experience that felt both comfortably familiar and refreshingly updated. Navigating the multiplayer battles was a challenge, but the reward was the exhilarating ability to seize moments of triumph. The introduction of the Skewer was a stroke of brilliance, adding in a weapon that instantly became one the all time best. Incredibly fun to use, while not being as annoying as something like the rocket launcher. Halo Infinite's multiplayer mode emerged as a surprising contender for the title of the best free multiplayer game ever. I still do not quite get why they made it free, but props to them. While I didn't delve into the story, the word on the grapevine was positive, suggesting a commendable narrative experience. Despite initial hesitations due to the absence of Bungie, the story seemed to have garnered its share of praise. The game's only substantial shortcoming was the longing for more content. The initial absence of Forge mode felt like a missed opportunity, but when it was eventually added, it transformed the game, akin to Garry's Mod in the Halo universe. Gunplay within the multiplayer was, in my view, the most enjoyable and captivating among all the Halo titles. The assortment of maps and weaponry was thoughtfully designed, with the "Breaker" map standing out as an instant favorite for me. Finally, the vehicles introduced a level of exhilaration that was unmatched. The sheer thrill of piloting these mechanical marvels added a dynamic layer to the gameplay that was nothing short of joyous. While they of course existed in past games, none quite did it like this one.
