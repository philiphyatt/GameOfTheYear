Middle-earth: Shadow of War




While the story mode and quests of Shadow of War may not have completely won me over, the game's diverse array of features compensates for any narrative shortcomings. The story was far from bad, I just found the missions dreadfully boring when compared to the fun of the open-world. They often felt lackluster and failed to hold my attention. However, this shortcoming is more than compensated for by the game's many multifaceted strengths. Combat stands as one of the game's pillars. The fluid and dynamic combat system ensures that every encounter feels like a captivating scene from a top-tier fantasy movie. The thrill of dispatching foes with an arsenal of abilities keeps the gameplay feeling fresh and engaging. Special mention goes to the teleport kills, a brilliant addition that offers a uniquely enjoyable way to eliminate enemies and traverse the map. The inclusion of animal combat and stealth mechanics adds layers of variety to the experience. Sneaking through the shadows and shrubbery or utilizing creatures to wreak havoc introduces tactical depth, catering to a diverse range of playstyles. Each acquired ability opens up new avenues for experimentation, making every encounter a chance to flex your strategic muscles. The heart of the game resides within the captivating Nemesis System, which breathes life into the ranks of the orc captains. Engaging in battles against these distinct personalities offers a fresh challenge each time, with each captain possessing their own unique strengths and weaknesses. I’ve played for years and I don’t think I have come anywhere close to seeing them all. The excitement of recruiting new captains is unparalleled, and the strategic depth added to the game is simply exhilarating. However, I found it annoying and counterintuitive that more and more captains have Iron Will as the game goes on and are unrecruitable. The Siege system is another aspect where the game truly shines, capturing the essence of epic warfare. The unpredictability and multitude of possible interactions make each siege an enthralling experience. The sheer variety of strategies and outcomes ensure that, even after years of playing, new surprises continue to emerge.
