EA Sports UFC 3




While UFC 1 & 2 just were not quite it, UFC 3 managed to capture the magic and round out the edges of what they were going for as a franchise. And while UFC 4 improved certain minor aspects, it was this third installment that truly hit the sweet spot, establishing itself as one of the most satisfying and captivating fighting games in recent memory. Unlike its predecessors, UFC 3 perfectly balanced the elements that make a fighting game shine. While UFC 4 felt more like an incremental update, refining grappling and submission mechanics, UFC 3 stands as a testament to the franchise's potential when the execution is on point. This title stands out as a personal favorite among fighting games, delivering a deeply rewarding experience that resonates with both hardcore fans and newcomers alike. Besides Mortal Kombat, I never really connected with fighting games and I believe it was because they were just too goofy and disconnected from reality. The UFC franchise are the first fighting games I have played that actually feel like real life, hand-to-hand combat. UFC 3 isn't just an individual pursuit; it shines brightest in the company of friends. Competing against buddies is definitely the way to go with this one, like most fighting games. The dynamic nature of each fight ensures that no two matches are the same, fostering intense rivalries and memorable moments. The roster is a treasure trove of current and classic fighters, offering ample choices for every fighting style preference. This variety ensures that players can find a fighter that resonates with their preferred tactics, adding another layer of strategy to the gameplay. While NBA 2K's MyCareer might have more popularity, UFC's career mode is by far my favorite of any sports game. The journey from an up-and-coming fighter to a legendary champion is immersive, challenging, and immensely satisfying. The icing on the cake lies in the robust character customization system. Crafting your fighter's appearance and style adds a personal touch, making every match a reflection of your choices and identity. You can make your guy into a badass, yet realistic, Dagestani brawler or just a straight up anime character.
